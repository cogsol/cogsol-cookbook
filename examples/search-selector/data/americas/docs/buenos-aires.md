# Buenos Aires, Argentina

The Paris of South America pulses with tango music, incredible steak, European-influenced architecture, and a passionate cultural spirit.

## Overview

Buenos Aires is the capital of Argentina, located on the western shore of the Rio de la Plata. With its wide avenues, ornate buildings, and café culture, the city has a distinctly European feel. Porteños, as the locals are called, are known for their warmth, late-night lifestyles, and deep love of football, tango, and asado.

## Top Attractions

- **La Boca**: A colorful riverside neighborhood famous for the Caminito street museum and Boca Juniors' stadium.
- **Recoleta Cemetery**: A stunning necropolis with elaborate mausoleums, including the tomb of Eva Perón.
- **Teatro Colón**: One of the world's finest opera houses, renowned for its acoustics and opulent design.
- **San Telmo**: A bohemian neighborhood known for its Sunday antiques fair, tango bars, and cobblestone streets.
- **Plaza de Mayo**: The historic central square flanked by the Casa Rosada presidential palace.

## Local Cuisine

Argentine cuisine revolves around beef. A traditional asado (barbecue) includes various cuts grilled slowly over wood embers, accompanied by chimichurri sauce. Try empanadas stuffed with meat or cheese, milanesa (breaded cutlet), choripán (chorizo sandwich), and dulce de leche in every dessert imaginable. The café culture is strong; enjoy a cortado with medialunas (sweet croissants).

## Best Time to Visit

March to May (autumn) and September to November (spring) offer mild temperatures and colorful scenery. Summer (December to February) is hot and many locals leave for vacation. Winter (June to August) is cool but rarely freezing.

## Travel Tips

- Carry Argentine pesos in cash; many smaller shops and restaurants do not accept cards.
- Dinner starts very late, typically around 9 or 10 PM; restaurants may not open before then.
- Learn basic Spanish; English is not widely spoken outside tourist areas.
- Take a tango lesson or watch a show in San Telmo or La Boca for an authentic experience.
- Use the Subte (subway) and colectivos (buses) to get around affordably.
