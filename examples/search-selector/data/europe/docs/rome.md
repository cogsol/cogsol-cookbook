# Rome, Italy

The Eternal City is a living museum where ancient ruins stand alongside Renaissance masterpieces and vibrant street life.

## Overview

Rome is the capital of Italy and one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. Founded in 753 BC, it was the heart of the Roman Empire and later became the center of the Catholic Church. Today it combines millennia of history with a lively modern Italian lifestyle.

## Top Attractions

- **Colosseum**: The largest ancient amphitheater ever built, once hosting gladiatorial contests.
- **Vatican City**: Home to St. Peter's Basilica, the Sistine Chapel, and the Vatican Museums.
- **Pantheon**: A remarkably preserved Roman temple with its famous open oculus dome.
- **Roman Forum**: The ruins of the ancient civic center of the Roman Empire.
- **Trevi Fountain**: A stunning Baroque fountain where visitors toss coins for good luck.

## Best Time to Visit

April to June and September to October offer pleasant weather and manageable crowds. July and August are hot and crowded but have a festive atmosphere. Winter is quiet with shorter lines at major sites.

## Travel Tips

- Book tickets for the Colosseum and Vatican Museums online in advance to skip long queues.
- Wear comfortable shoes; Rome is best explored on foot over its cobblestone streets.
- Beware of pickpockets near tourist hotspots and on public transportation.
- Restaurants near major landmarks tend to be overpriced; wander a few blocks for better value.
- Carry a refillable water bottle and use the many free public fountains throughout the city.
