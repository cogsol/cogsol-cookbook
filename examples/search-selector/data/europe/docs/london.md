# London, United Kingdom

A global metropolis blending centuries of royal history with cutting-edge culture, theater, and cuisine from every corner of the world.

## Overview

London is the capital of England and the United Kingdom, straddling the River Thames. With a population of over nine million, it is one of the world's most diverse cities. From Buckingham Palace to the modern Shard skyscraper, London seamlessly weaves tradition with innovation.

## Top Attractions

- **British Museum**: One of the world's greatest museums with free admission and millions of artifacts.
- **Tower of London**: A historic fortress housing the Crown Jewels and nearly a thousand years of history.
- **Buckingham Palace**: The official residence of the monarch, famous for the Changing of the Guard ceremony.
- **Westminster Abbey**: A Gothic church and the site of royal coronations since 1066.
- **South Bank**: A vibrant cultural strip along the Thames featuring the Tate Modern, Shakespeare's Globe, and the London Eye.

## Local Cuisine

London's food scene is remarkably international. Try a traditional full English breakfast, fish and chips from a proper chippy, a Sunday roast with Yorkshire pudding, and afternoon tea with scones and clotted cream. Borough Market is a food lover's paradise with stalls offering everything from artisan cheese to street food from dozens of cuisines.

## Best Time to Visit

Late spring (May to June) brings long days and pleasant temperatures. September offers mild weather after the summer rush. Summer is warm but crowded. Winter brings festive Christmas markets and lower hotel rates despite shorter days.

## Travel Tips

- Get an Oyster card or use contactless payment for the Tube and buses to save on fares.
- Many top museums and galleries, including the British Museum and National Gallery, are free.
- An umbrella or rain jacket is essential year-round; weather changes quickly.
- Stand on the right side of escalators and walk on the left; Londoners take this seriously.
- Explore beyond central London; neighborhoods like Camden, Greenwich, and Brixton have unique character.
