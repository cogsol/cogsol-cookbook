# Bangkok, Thailand

A sensory overload of ornate temples, bustling markets, legendary street food, and a nightlife scene that never sleeps.

## Overview

Bangkok is the capital of Thailand, a sprawling metropolis of over ten million people. Known in Thai as Krung Thep, the city mixes centuries-old Buddhist temples with modern skyscrapers and massive shopping malls. Its network of canals, vibrant street life, and affordable prices make it one of the most visited cities in the world.

## Top Attractions

- **Grand Palace**: A dazzling complex of royal buildings and the revered Emerald Buddha temple.
- **Wat Arun**: The Temple of Dawn, with its iconic riverside spire covered in colorful porcelain.
- **Chatuchak Weekend Market**: One of the world's largest outdoor markets with over 15,000 stalls.
- **Khao San Road**: A legendary backpacker hub known for its nightlife and street vendors.
- **Chinatown (Yaowarat)**: A vibrant neighborhood famous for gold shops and some of Bangkok's best street food.

## Local Cuisine

Bangkok is a street food paradise. Try pad thai cooked in a blazing wok, green and red curries with jasmine rice, som tum (papaya salad), mango sticky rice, and boat noodles from canal-side vendors. The city's night markets and food stalls offer full meals for just a few dollars. Michelin-recognized street food stalls like Jay Fai elevate simple dishes to legendary status.

## Best Time to Visit

November to February is the cool and dry season, ideal for sightseeing. March to May is the hot season with temperatures above 35 degrees Celsius. June to October is the rainy season, with heavy afternoon showers but lower prices.

## Travel Tips

- Use the BTS Skytrain and MRT Metro to avoid Bangkok's notorious traffic jams.
- Dress modestly when visiting temples: cover shoulders and knees.
- Always negotiate tuk-tuk fares before getting in, or use ride-hailing apps like Grab.
- Carry a small towel and stay hydrated; the heat and humidity can be intense.
- The Thai monarchy is deeply respected; always show respect when the topic arises.
