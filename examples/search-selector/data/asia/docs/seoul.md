# Seoul, South Korea

A dynamic capital where ancient palaces coexist with K-pop culture, cutting-edge technology, and some of Asia's most exciting food.

## Overview

Seoul is the capital of South Korea, home to nearly ten million people. The city straddles the Han River and is surrounded by mountains, creating a striking urban landscape. As a global hub for technology, entertainment, and fashion, Seoul perfectly balances its deep Confucian heritage with a relentlessly modern outlook.

## Top Attractions

- **Gyeongbokgung Palace**: The largest of Seoul's Five Grand Palaces, dating back to 1395.
- **Bukchon Hanok Village**: A traditional neighborhood of hundreds of hanok (Korean wooden houses).
- **Myeongdong**: A bustling shopping district famous for Korean beauty products and street food.
- **Namsan Tower**: An iconic tower on Namsan Mountain offering sweeping city views.
- **Hongdae**: A youthful neighborhood centered around Hongik University, known for indie music, street art, and nightlife.

## Local Cuisine

Korean food is bold, fermented, and deeply satisfying. Essential dishes include bibimbap (rice with mixed vegetables and gochujang), Korean BBQ grilled at your table, kimchi jjigae (kimchi stew), tteokbokki (spicy rice cakes), and jjajangmyeon (black bean noodles). Seoul's pojangmacha (street food tents) serve hot snacks late into the night.

## Best Time to Visit

Spring (April to May) brings cherry blossoms and mild temperatures. Autumn (September to November) is stunning with colorful foliage across the city's mountains. Summer is hot and humid with monsoon rains. Winter is cold but festive, with ski resorts nearby.

## Travel Tips

- Get a T-money card for easy access to Seoul's extensive and affordable subway system.
- Free Wi-Fi is available nearly everywhere, including subway stations and buses.
- Remove your shoes when entering traditional restaurants, homes, and some shops.
- Download a translation app; English signage is common but not universal outside tourist areas.
- Visit a jjimjilbang (Korean bathhouse) for an authentic cultural experience.
