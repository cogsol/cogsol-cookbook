# Tokyo, Japan

A futuristic megacity where ancient temples sit beneath neon-lit skyscrapers and culinary excellence reaches its highest form.

## Overview

Tokyo is the capital of Japan and the most populous metropolitan area in the world. The city is a fascinating contrast of ultra-modern technology and deep-rooted tradition. Each of its distinct neighborhoods has its own personality, from the electronics bazaar of Akihabara to the serene gardens of Shinjuku Gyoen.

## Top Attractions

- **Senso-ji Temple**: Tokyo's oldest Buddhist temple in the historic Asakusa district.
- **Shibuya Crossing**: The world's busiest pedestrian intersection, a symbol of Tokyo's energy.
- **Meiji Shrine**: A tranquil Shinto shrine surrounded by a lush forest in the heart of the city.
- **Tsukiji Outer Market**: A bustling marketplace for the freshest sushi, seafood, and Japanese snacks.
- **Tokyo Skytree**: The tallest tower in Japan with observation decks offering views of the entire metropolitan area.

## Local Cuisine

Tokyo has the most Michelin-starred restaurants of any city in the world. Must-try experiences include fresh sushi at a counter-style restaurant, tonkotsu or shoyu ramen from a neighborhood shop, tempura, yakitori grilled over charcoal, and Japanese wagyu beef. Convenience stores offer surprisingly high-quality onigiri and bento boxes.

## Best Time to Visit

Late March to mid-April is cherry blossom season, a magical time to visit. Autumn (October to November) brings vibrant fall colors and comfortable temperatures. Summer is hot and humid, while winter is cold but sunny with fewer tourists.

## Travel Tips

- Get a Suica or Pasmo IC card for seamless travel on trains and buses.
- Learn basic Japanese greetings; a bow goes a long way in showing respect.
- Cash is still widely used despite the city's high-tech reputation; carry yen.
- Tipping is not customary and can even be considered rude.
- Trains are extremely punctual; plan journeys using apps like Google Maps or Navitime.
