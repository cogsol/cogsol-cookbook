# Vegetable Stir-Fry

A colorful mix of fresh vegetables cooked quickly in a savory sauce with garlic and ginger. This vegetarian dish is fast, healthy, and endlessly customizable. Serve over steamed rice or noodles.

## Ingredients

### Vegetables
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced
- 150g broccoli florets
- 1 medium carrot, julienned
- 150g snap peas or snow peas
- 100g mushrooms, sliced
- 1 small zucchini, sliced into half-moons
- 2 green onions, sliced

### Sauce
- 3 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon sesame oil
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon honey or brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, grated
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)

### For Cooking
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- Sesame seeds for garnish
- Steamed rice or noodles for serving

## Instructions

1. Mix all the sauce ingredients in a small bowl and set aside.
2. Heat the vegetable oil in a large wok or skillet over high heat until it shimmers.
3. Add the carrots and broccoli first (they take longest to cook). Stir-fry for 2 minutes.
4. Add the bell peppers, snap peas, mushrooms, and zucchini. Stir-fry for 3 minutes, keeping the vegetables moving constantly.
5. Add the garlic and ginger. Cook for 30 seconds until fragrant.
6. Pour the sauce over the vegetables. Toss to coat evenly and cook for 1 minute until the sauce thickens.
7. Remove from heat. Top with green onions and sesame seeds.
8. Serve immediately over steamed rice or noodles.

## Details

- Preparation time: 15 minutes
- Cooking time: 7 minutes
- Difficulty: Easy
- Servings: 4
- Cuisine: Asian
- Type: Main course (vegetarian)
